Management - Arts, Science Profession

  • Management as a Science

    • Science refers to a systematic body of knowledge to understand phenomena and management as a science has (i) cause and effect relationship (ii) principles should be verifiable. (iii) predictable results. (iv) universal application.

    • Theories and techniques like, PERT and CPM, break even analysis, budgeting etc. are all scientific in nature.

  • Management as an Art:

    • Management deals with human beings, we cannot predict a definite cause - effect relationship. Hence it is not treated as a pure science.

    • Art refers to application of skill. It is creative and improves by practice. Art of managing varies from one manager to another with experience.

    • Thus, management is a combination of both science and art.

  • Management as Profession: Any occupation is a profession if it has (i) systematic knowledge like engineering or chartered accountancy (ii) formal method to acquire knowledge with a degree and certificate (iii) association to devise code of conduct for professionals (iv) financial reward should not be the main measure of success but to serve the long-run interests of the society. All India Management Association in India is representative of managers and devising codes of conduct for managers.
